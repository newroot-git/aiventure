Get out of the group chat. One AI that knows your crew, knows what's on, and tells you what to actually go do — together.

01Thesis

Friend groups don't fail to hang out because they don't want to. They fail because deciding what, when, and with who is admin hell — and the group chat is the worst possible tool for it. Touch Grass replaces that admin with one agent that turns intent into a real plan.

The core move

You give it people + a loose intent + your bounds (time, money, distance, novelty). It hands back a few real, grounded things to go do — tuned to what your group actually likes.

Why now

AI can finally do the messy part — merge several people's tastes and ground suggestions in live local data. That wasn't possible two years ago.

The compounding bit

Every plan you run teaches it your crew. Over time "Conor, Jack, Saturday" → four great options instantly. The history is the product.

02The problem is real and visceral

This isn't a productivity gap. It's a connection gap — which is why it's worth leading the marketing with emotion, not features.

"idk, what do you wanna do?" — the four words that kill the weekend.

  • The "easygoing" deadlock. Everyone says "anywhere's fine" to seem chill. Research (Duke): the people who never pick get invited out less. Politeness quietly erodes the friendship.
  • No one owns it. Bigger group = less individual ownership (diffusion of responsibility). The bigger the chat, the less likely anyone locks the plan.
  • Death by a thousand apps. When2Meet for time, a poll for place, Splitwise for money, a shared album for photos. Nothing talks to anything.
  • The planner friend burns out. One person absorbs all the herding — times, place, money, reminders — and slowly resents it.

Lead line for the landing page →  "Stop saying 'we should hang out more.' Just hang out."   (Hook with the funny one — "idk what do you wanna do" — anchor the meaning in the loneliness data.)

03The white space — and the graveyard around it

The exact idea is unoccupied at scale. But the neighbourhood is littered with corpses. Both facts matter.

Unclaimed

No one does this

Every funded player chases making new friends (Pie, Timeleft, Clyx) or trip planning (Mindtrip, Troupe, Wanderlog). Nobody tells your existing group what to do from combined interests + history. The "this Saturday ↔ big trip" seam — basically empty.

Dangerous

Why they died

IRL (95% bots, SEC fraud), Cluster, Squad, Down. Three killers: the whole-group install tax, iMessage gravity (free, everyone's already there), and utility ≠ desire (coordinators are "vegetables" — low pull, retention decays).

The AI unlock that beats all three killers

  • Deliver value from one person's input before the whole group joins. AI can suggest off your profile alone → defuses the install tax that murdered everyone else.
  • The moat is the per-group taste + outcome history, not the suggestions. "AI suggests stuff" is cloneable in a weekend; a compounding record of what your crew liked, vetoed and did is not.
  • Grounding is trust. Ungrounded AI hallucinates closed restaurants and impossible geography. Real data + forced source citations = the difference between a toy and a product.

04The engine — one loop, any scale

Your five "levels" are not five products. They're one planning agent over {who} + {intent} + {constraints} that can start from any known vertex and fill in the rest.

MICROtonight, low stakes

"What movie do we watch / game do we play tonight?" — same engine, tiny bounds.

SOLOjust you

"What should I do today?" — recs off your own interests + local options.

PAIRa date / 1:1

Merge two interest profiles → an itinerary for a date or catch-up.

GROUPthe crew, a day

"Something with the boys Saturday" → 3–4 grounded options the group votes on. ← the wedge.

MACROa whole trip

"Somewhere 2–4hrs away this summer" → a full multi-day plan from the group's shared tastes.

Start from any vertex — the agent fills the gaps

Know WHAT, not WHO

"I wanna golf today — who's keen?" → finds the crew likely to bite + a real tee time.

Know WHO, not WHAT

"Something with Conor & Jack Saturday" → options tuned to all three.

Know NOTHING

"I want to do something Saturday" → surfaces both a plan and the people for it.

Know the BOUNDS

"Somewhere we can fly to for 2 weeks in summer" → destinations first, then who's in.

Two ways options appear (Conor's point — not everything is AI)

AI-generate

The Drop

Agent researches + proposes real options. The wow moment, the premium feature.

Human-drop

Throw it in the ring

Anyone pastes a course / film / bar they're keen on; app runs the vote or randomiser. Cheap, keeps humans in the loop, covers cases too deep for AI.

05The wedge & the moat

Every winner in this space narrowed to one sharp recurring job (Partiful = invites, Timeleft = Wednesday dinners). Going broad on day one is the IRL death. So we pick one and own it.

Own this first

The weekend group plan

"What should <crew> do this weekend?" High frequency (weekly, not 2×/year like trips), instantly relatable, and the magic demo. Earn the weekly habit, then graduate the same crew + taste graph into the occasional big trip — the retention prize no trip-app can match.

Defend with this

The taste & history graph

Per-group preferences + what you did + what you vetoed, compounding every plan. Suggestions are cloneable; this isn't. Treat it as the actual product — the suggestions are just its surface.

07Build stack

Repo quality is 30% of the score (AI-graded). Grounding is what makes it feel real. ~30 min of API signups buys the whole thing.

AppNext.js (App Router) on VercelFast to ship, clean repo, instant deploy URL for the demo-link field.

SkipYelp · Foursquare · Eventbrite · Meetup · AllTrailsPaywalled, locked-down, or dead. Each is a 2.5-day time sink.

09How it makes money (the AI-cost tension, resolved)

You flagged it: heavy AI = paid. Resolution is freemium that mirrors the research — coordination is table stakes (free, drives the network), AI planning is the premium people pay for.

Free

Crews, profiles, human-drop voting, basic coordination, a few AI Drops/month. The WhatsApp-killer utility that pulls the whole group in.

Plus

Unlimited Drops, deeper personalisation off the history graph, novelty engine, the date/pair mode.

Later

Trip mode (multi-day itineraries), affiliate/booking commission on tee times, tickets, tables — the OTAs' real game, but as a layer not the headline.

10Open decisions to lock before/while building

1 · Name — Touch Grass vs Out of the Group Chat?
"Touch Grass" = punchy, memey, screenshot-friendly (good for the community vote). "Out of the Group Chat" = the positioning line.
Lean: Touch Grass as the name, "get out of the group chat" as the tagline. Best of both.
2 · Demo location — where's the crew based?
Grounding quality varies by city. Ticketmaster + Google Places are global; pick a city you both know so you can sanity-check that suggestions are genuinely good on stage.
Lean: a UK city you know well (verifiable on stage) — or Singapore for judge-resonance. Decide Wed.
3 · IP / repo license
Hackathon ToS is gated behind signup (no public terms found). AI grader wants a repo link, not necessarily open source.
Lean: confirm terms at signup, license repo All Rights Reserved, keep core prompts/engine private. Email organisers if unclear.
4 · How much AI in the MVP?
Full AI-generate is the wow but costs more; human-drop voting is cheap.
Lean: MVP leads with AI-generate (The Drop) — it's the differentiator and the demo. Human-drop = v2.
