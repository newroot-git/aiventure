# AIventure — Competitor Research

_Researched June 2026. Four-cluster sweep: planning/logistics, AI hangout planners, IRL social, anti-social-media._

AIventure = existing friend groups plan real-world adventures together. Slots, voting, RSVP, shared availability. AI agent helps plan. Anti-social-media ethos. Brand: "Earthbound & Stargazing."

---

## TL;DR — where AIventure actually sits

The market splits on two axes:

1. **Meet strangers** (Timeleft, 222, Pie, Joiner, Bumble BFF, Kndrd) vs **organize existing friends** (Howbout, Partiful, Geneva, Pals). → **AIventure is existing-friends.** Less VC saturation, but weaker proven willingness-to-pay.
2. **Find a date** (When2meet, Rallly, Doodle, Howbout) vs **set a date / invite** (Partiful, Luma) vs **all-in-one** (Howbout, Pals, GetTogether). → AIventure is all-in-one.

**The white space (validated by all 4 agents): nobody ships genuinely agentic AI that GENERATES a real, bookable group outing AND runs best-in-class coordination (slots/voting/RSVP/availability) AND works in any city — under a positive, anti-feed "go do something worth remembering" brand.** Each competitor owns one piece; none own the synthesis.

## Cluster 1 — Planning / logistics

| App | What | Scale | Gap vs us |
|-----|------|-------|-----------|
| **Howbout** | Shared friend calendar; availability-aware voting + polls + chat + bucket lists. **Closest direct competitor.** | ~6M downloads, 4.8★, free, "dark social" (no feed) | No AI planning, no adventure curation, no anti-feed brand story. Notification overload = #1 complaint |
| **Partiful** | "Facebook events for hot people." Beautiful invites, SMS-native reminders, no-app RSVP. | ~500k MAU, +400% YoY, $27.3M raised, ~$100M val (a16z) | Built for ONE host throwing a party; no availability-finding or option voting. Date is assumed fixed |
| **Luma** | Beautiful event pages, ticketing, QR check-in. Host→audience. | Big in tech/community events | Only proven consumer monetization in the set: ~5% ticketing fee + $39–150/mo Plus. Not friend-group coordination |
| **Meetup** | Interest-group discovery, recurring events. | 35M+ members | Legacy, organizer-pays, pricing backlash (costs ~3×'d 2024). Wrong tool for private circles |
| **When2meet** | No-login availability grid. The "good enough" default. | Huge usage, ugly/dated | No mobile app, no reminders, no social layer. **Ripe for a polished mobile-native kill** |
| **Rallly / Doodle / Cal.com** | Date polls / booking links. Pro-leaning. | Doodle millions; Cal 1M+ | Narrow utilities; business vibe, not friend adventures |

**2025–26 newcomer wave (most direct):** **Pals** (pals.social — "WhatsApp+Eventbrite+Yelp," map-first, AI venue discovery, Going/Pass voting, itineraries, task assignment), **GetTogether** (date polls + venue picking + AI itinerary suggestions), **Anout** ("plans that actually happen," spontaneity-first), **OurCal** (group calendar, 100k+ groups). These converge on our exact spec — **"we do all the steps" is now table stakes, not differentiation.**

## Cluster 2 — AI hangout planners

| App | AI quality | Scale | Read |
|-----|-----------|-------|------|
| **Gather: Plan & Hangout with AI** | Thin — NL event autofill + chat summary. Gimmick. | 3 ratings, dormant since 2024 | Closest NAME/concept match, basically abandoned. Leaves the lane open |
| **Gallivant** | Not real AI — editorial curation dressed as personalization | 40 ratings, NYC-only | Best feature-overlap: "Journey Cards" (1–3 stop itineraries) + invite + booking + memories feed. **Steal the 1–3 stop journey primitive.** Doesn't scale (hand-curated, single city) |
| **Pie** (Andy Dunn) | Genuinely AI-core — ChatGPT groups compatible strangers | $11.5M Series A, ~130k MAU, 4 cities | AI matches PEOPLE not PLANS. Hands-on reviews: "thin directory," most users found events elsewhere |
| **Pals** | Light AI venue suggestion | Unknown, early | Voting + itinerary + AI suggestion combo — watch closely |

**Verdict: AI in this space is almost entirely veneer.** Only Pie is AI-core, and it matches people, not outings. **"Agentic AI that generates + plans the whole group outing" is essentially unoccupied.** This is the wedge.

## Cluster 3 — IRL social (mostly meet-strangers)

- **Timeleft** [strangers] — curated 6-stranger dinners. ~€18M ARR, 150k monthly, 200+ cities. Cracked cold-start ("151 signups before launch"). Billing-complaint horror stories.
- **222** [strangers] — AI meet-cute matching. $13.7M raised, YC. Forced contact-sync + "invite 5 friends" gate = trust complaints.
- **Pie** [strangers] — see above.
- **Bumble BFF / Geneva** — Bumble admits BFF "hasn't worked." **Geneva** (the best existing-friends group+event+RSVP engine, ~$36M raised) is **being shut down** post-acquisition → **vacates the dedicated group-planning lane right as we enter.**
- **Posh** [events] — "what are we doing tonight," TikTok-for-events. $37M Series B, $350M GMV, ticketing model (10%+$0.99).
- **Yubo** [strangers, online] — 85M Gen Z, livestream not IRL.
- **Fabriq / Garden** [existing friends] — relationship-CRM "stay in touch." Solo-side, no group coordination, but clean anti-social-media ethos precedent.
- **Kndrd** [strangers] — women-only IRL, NYC. "Spots" mechanic ≈ our slots.

**Structural advantage we should foreground:** stranger-apps' hardest problem is liquidity/cold-start. **We activate closed friend graphs — they bring their own liquidity.** We sidestep the marketplace problem entirely.

## Cluster 4 — Anti-social-media

**Two sub-types behave very differently:**

- **Anti-feed social apps (a graveyard):** BeReal (73.5M MAU peak Aug 2022 → -61% DAU in 5 months → never monetized, bled $3M/mo → fire-sold to Voodoo €500M). Minus (art provocation, 100-posts-for-life countdown). Somewhere Good (values-first, stayed tiny, wound down). Retro (survived by being weekly + low-pressure). **Touch Grass Connect / Together** — our closest ethos competitors (create real-life hangouts; "beating brainrot, breaking the feed"). Jokey/shallow tone.
- **Friction tools (the healthy businesses):** **Opal** (~$10M ARR, 11 people, charges $99/yr from day one), **one sec** (PNAS study: -57% app opens; friction > blocking), **Clearspace** (do pushups to unlock), **Roots** (communal detox challenges with friends).

**The core tension to design around: an app fighting screen time fights itself.** Anti-feed social apps die on network-effects + novelty decay (BeReal). Friction tools survive because success ISN'T measured in DAU — they're utilities on subscription.

**Trend 2025–26:** dumbphone sales +25%, "touch grass" went meme→product category, friction tools compounding, "verified real-world action" + "communal detox" are the live energy. **This is our lane and it's culturally ascendant.**

## What to STEAL

1. **Gallivant's "Journey Card"** — the 1–3 stop outing as the core planning primitive. Clean, copyable, scales with LLM generation where Gallivant can't (it's hand-curated).
2. **Howbout's availability-aware voting** — when you vote on an option, your availability auto-shows. Table-stakes mechanic, must be excellent.
3. **Partiful's SMS-native, no-app-required RSVP** — frictionless invite; guests don't need to install.
4. **Opal/one sec's "charge from day one"** — a wellbeing mission needs subscription. Free-and-ad = the BeReal death trap.
5. **Minus's countdown + Opal's "time saved"** — but reframe as a POSITIVE metric: "adventures had together," not session length.
6. **Roots' communal challenge** — detox/adventure WITH friends beats solo willpower. Our built-in advantage.
7. **one sec's published evidence** — behavior-change proof is both marketing and moat.
8. **Restrained, AI-prioritized notifications** — Howbout's #1 complaint is notification overload. Smart nudges = clear opening.

## What to AVOID

- **No business model** (BeReal). Charge early.
- **Daily-obligation mechanics** → novelty → chore → churn.
- **Authenticity-as-product** → invites the performance it claims to kill.
- **Forced contact-sync / invite-gates** (222) → trust killer. Don't harvest contacts to unlock core utility.
- **Silent auto-renew / refund failures** (Timeleft) → out-trust incumbents with transparent billing.
- **Hand-curated, single-city content** (Gallivant, Pie) → geography is the silent killer. Build to work in ANY city (LLM + Places API).
- **Selling abstinence** ("stop using your phone," negative/willpower-framed) → sell the positive: "go do something worth remembering, with people you love." Phone reduction is the side effect, never the headline.

## Positioning conclusion

**"Agentic AI that generates a real group outing (Gallivant-style 1–3 stops) for any city, then runs Howbout-grade voting/RSVP/availability on top — wrapped in a sincere, anti-feed 'go make a memory' brand."**

- Pie proves AI-core + loneliness narrative raises money.
- Howbout proves coordination must be excellent and free.
- Gallivant proves the itinerary+booking+memories loop resonates but won't scale hand-curated.
- Opal proves charge-from-day-one is survivable and right for a wellbeing mission.

None ship the synthesis. **Differentiation ≠ the mechanics (commoditized). Differentiation = real planning AI + positive anti-feed brand + works-anywhere.**

**Brand whitespace:** the meme players (Touch Grass) are jokey/shallow; the wellness players (Opal/Calm) are clinical/solo. **Nobody owns sincere wonder + grounded warmth + real friends + real adventure.** "Earthbound & Stargazing" can be earnest-not-preachy, communal-not-solo, adventurous-not-abstinent. That's open.

### Open strategic questions
- **Monetization:** ticketing rake (Luma/Posh) vs premium group features vs venue/booking affiliate (fits AI-discovery). Existing-friends lane has weaker proven willingness-to-pay than the stranger-dinner lane — real risk.
- **Availability model:** frictionless manual slots (When2meet — viral, shallow) vs calendar sync (Howbout — sticky, hard cold-start). Probably hedge: manual tap-your-dates with optional sync.
- **Naming:** "Gallivant" is taken (direct competitor, App Store + gallivant.app). "Jaunt" crowded (mostly mobility). Hunt cleaner: Lark, Junket, or coinage.
